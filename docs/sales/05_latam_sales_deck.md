## Latin America Overview

LatAm is expected to see an influx of 32 million new digital consumers by 2030. The region is the world leader in time spent online.

Cross-border digital commerce currently stands at USD 70 billion, nearly doubling in just 2 years. By 2026, the XB market will soar to USD 144 billion, showcasing a CAGR of 27%. Historical connections, language influence, and similar consumer behaviors support a shared cultural foundation making it easier for world-famous brands to resonate with Latin American consumers.

## Latin America — Payments Landscape

### Cards vs. APMs

In the payments landscape, cards lead for online transactions, with debit and credit cards having 58% of market share. APMs maintain significant relevance and their importance is poised to grow, mostly pushed by the expansion of instant transfers. In most countries, employing a blend of cards and APMs is a pivotal strategy for broadening audience reach. APMs account for 42% of LatAm's digital commerce in 2023.

Card ownership jumped from 33% to 57% between 2011 and 2021. In digital commerce, credit cards command 48% of the market and debit cards hold 10%. Domestic cards (credit and debit) represent most of the volume; installments are an important avenue for higher-ticket purchases.

### Fastest-Growing Payment Methods (CAGR 2023–2026)

| Method | CAGR | Key Driver |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Pix & account-based transfers | 33% | Real-time confirmation, zero cost to consumers |
| Debit cards | 30% | Financial inclusion; more consumers accessing bank services |
| Digital wallets | 20% | Dominant in Argentina (~25% of sales) and Uruguay/Bolivia (~15%) |

### Alternative Payment Methods

- Pix (Brazil) and PSE (Colombia) are the most used instant transfers in LatAm digital commerce.
- Together with digital wallets, instant payments represent 30% of digital commerce.
- In Argentina, Mercado Pago digital wallets have a 23% share of digital commerce.
- In Peru, PagoEfectivo and cash vouchers account for nearly 20% of online sales.
- Wallets currently hold 9% of LatAm's market (USD 45B in 2023); by 2026 market size will be USD 77.4B (20% CAGR).
- Main wallet players: MercadoPago (7 countries incl. Brazil & Argentina), PayPal, MACHPay (Chile), PagoEfectivo (Peru).
- Cash-based payments are becoming less popular as instant A2A transfers (Pix, SPEI MX, PSE CO) expand.

*Source: PCMI 2023*

### Key Merchant Challenges in Argentina

| Challenge | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Complex FX & dual-rate environment | MULC (official rate) vs. CCL (alternative rate) creates settlement unpredictability; cost exposure and cross-border operational risk. |
| Hidden cost of non-local checkout | Processing cross-border cards without local routing triggers tax surcharges and unfavorable FX spreads (30–53%) — inflating final prices and reducing conversion. |
| Fragmented rails & limited coverage | Wallets and A2A are fastest-growing (17–25% CAGR) yet most global PSPs lack direct local connectivity. Without integrations into COELSA* and Mercado Pago, merchants face coverage gaps and decline spikes. |
| Mandatory tax orchestration | Certain taxes must be added, collected, and remitted at checkout. Failing to automate this through a licensed partner leads to massive tax exposure and manual compliance overhead. |

*COELSA = Argentina's central electronic clearing house*

### FX & Settlement in Argentina

| Mechanism | Description | Best For |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| MULC (Official rate) | Central Bank–regulated ARS → foreign currency remittance abroad | Streaming (6 business day settlement); digital goods/services qualifying under official FX. Remittance direct to merchant's country. |
| CCL (Alternative rate) | Market-based FX mechanism for foreign currency outside official channel | Travel and online retail (only option); can be interesting for digital goods and education. Remittance via Singapore. |

## Uruguay & Paraguay

### Regional Overview

Uruguay and Paraguay boast remarkably stable economies with low-to-moderate inflation. Uruguay has open foreign trade policies; Paraguay is renowned for favorable tax regulations. Both countries are experiencing surging digital commerce (25% CAGR 2023–2026). More than half of the adult population in both countries has made or received digital payments; 73% buys online. Between 2021 and 2023, digital buyers surged 83% and digital commerce market grew 64%. Cross-border sales: from USD 400M in 2020 to USD 1.9B in 2023 (29% CAGR 2023–2026). Leading XB verticals: streaming, gaming, online retail.

*Sources: UN; PCMI 2023*

### Uruguay

Uruguay has the highest consumer spending in all Latin America: USD 13,561 (double the regional average); 4th place for online spending per capita in LatAm. Digital commerce is worth USD 3.5B, with 24% average growth rate (2023–2026). 71% of Uruguayan adults buy online.

**Key payment facts:**
- Users pay up to 3.66% in additional fees if the transaction is international; with EBANX local solution, no extra fees.
- Installments play a crucial role: nearly 60% of all credit card online volume is paid in installments (local only).
- Debit card purchases benefit from a 2% reduction in IVA fees.
- Each resident has the right to make three annual purchases from abroad without taxes (paid with cards, max USD 200/purchase).
- Uruguayans commonly pay in USD for medium/high-ticket items; easy to open USD bank accounts.

### Paraguay

54% of Paraguayan adults own financial accounts (higher than Mexico's 49%); 51% have made or received digital payments. Digital commerce is worth USD 600M, with 33% CAGR (2023–2026) — third-highest in LatAm. Digital commerce penetration skyrocketed from 25% in 2021 to 74% in 2023, driven by a young population (median age 25 vs. 30 for LatAm).

**Key payment facts:**
- Paraguay has the highest cross-border share in South America: 77% of sales.
